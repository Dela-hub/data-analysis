Setup: a historic 8-week spike

Brent rose from $62.54 in Dec 2025 to $124.00 by Apr 2026 — a 98% surge in four months. This is the highest level in the 28-month dataset and well above the 2024 annual average of $81/bbl. The spike was driven by a sharp acceleration beginning in March.

Conflict: the pullback starts immediately

The EIA's official outlook puts Brent at $104 by July — a $20 decline in just 3 months. Every month you wait, the reference price you negotiate against falls. The asymmetry favours acting in May, not June. The Brent–WTI spread is $15/bbl — if your contracts reference both benchmarks, that differential is material cost exposure.
